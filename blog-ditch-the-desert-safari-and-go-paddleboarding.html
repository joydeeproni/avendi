Ditch* the Desert Safari and go Paddleboarding
  • By: Erica
  • Jun 17, 2019

*Desert Safari is awesome, but you should not miss out on a trending, awesome experience the city has to offer.
You gotta travel like a local :)

Via Instagram | @surfhousedubai

Think excursions in Dubai, and it won’t be look before you come up with a Desert Safari. Being sped across red dunes in a 4WD, watching a line of camels silhouetted against the sunset followed by belly dancing and a BBQ is a great way to pass a few hours. But if you are looking for a more authentic “live like a local” experience, then there is another way.

Dubai and the United Arab Emirates hosts some of the best paddle boarding locations in the region. It might be known for its deserts, but warm, consistently flat water and endless summer weather make it like nowhere else in the world. Tried and tested by the City’s fitness personalities, stand up paddle boarding (SUP) is Dubai’s favorite, new water sport. A quick, ten-minute tutorial is all beginners need to get out on the water.

Like ice skating in Central Park, paddle board in Dubai and you get a unique, panoramic view of the City from an unusual, calm perspective. The skyline of this incredible old meets new city is breath-taking, and from a paddleboard, you can stand on a platform of aquamarine and marvel.

Start a Weekend in Dubai like the locals

Get up early on Friday and head to the less touristy beach next to Dubai Offshore Sailing Club, and you can enjoy early morning paddle boarding with the locals.

Via Facebook | The Farmers’ Market On The Terrace

As the day starts to heat up, head to buy exotic fruit from one of the city’s Farmers Markets that have become trendy for locals to eat the first fruits of Dubai’s new organic farming movement and spend an hour doing yoga on the terrace.

When is the best time to paddleboard in Dubai?

The most consistent conditions for paddle boarding in Dubai and the United Emirates fall between October and June.

Dubai starts to cool down rapidly from September onwards and in November, December and January (peak season) temperatures are about 25, still warm beach weather and great for water sports and swimming in the sea.

It starts to heat up in Dubai by March with daily averages of 32. This is fantastic beach weather and the days are beginning to get longer so you can explore the city, late afternoon after a day on the water.

The best free beaches to try paddle boarding in Dubai

Dubai has some of the best beaches in the UAE. Although many of the beaches are affiliated with hotels and accessible only to their guests or paid visitors, there are some beautiful public beaches with clean sand, showers and changing rooms which are free.

  • Umm Suqeim Beach — Known for its stunning views of Burj Al Arab, this is a popular beach for water sports with clear water and a fairly constant cooling breeze. The beach provides changing huts and shower facilities and is a favorite with Jumeirah residents and Western expatriates. Approximately 1.6 kilometers from the downtown area and easily accessible by car or public transport, the nearest Metro Station is First Gulf Bank on the Red Line.
  • Kite Beach (Wollongong Beach) — This beach is a favorite with paddle boarders, kayakers and soccer and volleyball players who flock here to enjoy the warm water and long expanse of sand. Also known as Wollongong Beach as it is located just behind the University of Wollongong. When the tide is out, you can walk along the stretch of sand and enjoy stunning views of Burj Al Arab.
    The beach has no facilities so you will need to bring your own beach necessities. You can find Kite Beach opposite the Al Manara Road Junction or just after the World Project Office.
  • Black Palace Beach — The Al Surfouth (Black Palace Beach) is a beautiful, private beach with gorgeous views. Free from action junkies it is a great place to just kick back and relax. Easy to miss as it’s hidden amidst an imposing cover of 5-Star Hotels you can find the beach between Palm Jumeirah and Burj Al Arab.

Paddleboarding — Learn and Hire in Dubai

Via Facebook | IGNITE Surface

With some fantastic options of areas to stand up and paddle in Dubai, this is an incredible place to try SUP for the first time. There are some excellent schools offering equipment hire and lessons in areas where the water is calm and safe.

  • Surf House — Established in 2005, Surf House Dubai — Surf and SUP school is the UAE’s original surf and stand up paddle school. You can hire boards that are safe, stable and suitable for beginners plus wetsuits and rash vests if you need them and you can get a great breakfast and coffee when you’ve finished. Lessons are tailored for beginners through to advanced levels and for a private lesson you can expect to pay AED 275 or for a group of 3 AED 195 per person.
  • Ignite Surface — The team at Riva Beach Club have equipment for hire and lessons to suit all abilities. Riva Beach boasts the calmest SUP and Kayak waters in Dubai, making it a great beginner spot. When you have gained some confidence, you can race your friends to the Rixos Hotel opposite. Ignite have a handy booking app, and you can rent a board from AED 95 and have either a private or group lesson from AED 130.

The best way to experience local life in a new place is to meet people and visit some of the districts which are not catering specifically for tourists. Of course, Desert Safari itself is a magical experience (check out Olivia’s experience here). Yet, you should not miss out on what is trending amongst the locals today. Paddleboarding is a fantastic way to meet and interact with locals, and enjoy seeing this incredible city from a different perspective.
